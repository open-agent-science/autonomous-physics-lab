# CLAIM-0007: Fixed Standard Koide Target Fails Cross-Family Survival in the Falsifier MVP

## Statement

In the stored particle-mass relation falsifier MVP, the fixed standard Koide
target `Q = 2/3` does not survive the encoded charged-fermion family survey.
The charged-lepton triplet remains within propagated uncertainty, but the
up-type and down-type quark triplets miss the same fixed target outside
propagated uncertainty.

## Evidence Status

`RESULT-0011` provides a first falsifier-MVP result. This claim remains
`DRAFT` until maintainer review confirms the scope, wording, and benchmark
interpretation.

## Scope

- Fixed standard Koide relation only.
- Encoded within-family charged fermion triplets only:
  charged leptons, up-type quarks, and down-type quarks.
- Charged leptons use stored PDG 2025 pole masses.
- Quarks use stored PDG 2024 values with documented mixed scheme and scale
  limitations.
- The run includes uncertainty propagation, deterministic random-baseline
  calibration, and a fixed low complexity-penalty ledger.

## Caution

This negative result does not rule out every modified Koide-like relation,
common-scale quark reformulation, phase extension, alternate target value, or
future search family. It only falsifies cross-family survival of the fixed
standard target under the encoded assumptions.
